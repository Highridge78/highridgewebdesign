# Issue Language Bank (Why It Matters)

Use this to explain issues in commercial terms, not design opinions.

## Messaging / Clarity
- "A first-time visitor has to work too hard to understand what you do. That uncertainty creates drop-off before they become a lead."
- "Your headline describes a service category, but not a business outcome. Buyers convert faster when outcomes are explicit."

## CTA / Conversion
- "Your primary call-to-action is buried. Ready buyers need a clear action path in the first screen."
- "Multiple competing CTAs dilute intent. One primary action usually increases conversion rate."

## Trust / Credibility
- "You likely do strong work, but the site does not prove it quickly enough. Online trust has to be explicit, not implied."
- "Lack of trust indicators forces comparison shopping instead of confident contact."

## Mobile UX
- "Most local intent traffic is mobile; friction here has direct lead cost."
- "Tap targets and spacing create avoidable effort at the point of action."

## Performance
- "Slow first load increases abandonment before your offer is even seen."
- "Heavy visual behavior creates friction on weaker mobile devices, which often represent high-intent local traffic."

## SEO / Local
- "Without clear service + location relevance, search engines and buyers both struggle to match your page to intent."
- "Thin service detail limits visibility for bottom-of-funnel local searches."

## Strategic Direction Phrases
- "This is fixable with better page structure, clearer conversion hierarchy, and stronger trust sequencing."
- "The opportunity is not cosmetic. It is making the site operate like a sales asset."
